Alternatives to the three-dot menu

Six different ways to expose Ad Group actions — pick one or mix.

Option 1

Inline icon toolbar

Common actions live as labeled icon buttons in the card header. No menu — actions are one click away.

Ad Group 1 Total Variations: 9 Active
Rename Duplicate Pause Archive
Delete
  • Zero clicks to common actions
  • Five icons add visual noise on long lists
  • Tooltips clarify intent on hover
  • Caps you at ~5 actions before it gets crowded
Option 2

Split button (primary + more)

Promote one action ("Edit") to a primary button, hide the rest under a chevron.

Ad Group 1 Total Variations: 9
  • Clear primary action; less ambiguous than dots
  • Forces one action to be "the" primary
  • Compact — one control, two affordances
  • Secondary chevron still hides things
Option 3 · Recommended

"Actions" labeled dropdown

Same dropdown content, but the trigger is labeled — no guessing what the dots mean.

Ad Group 1 Total Variations: 9 Active
  • Discoverable — the label itself invites clicking
  • Slightly more horizontal space than dots
  • Holds as many actions as you want behind one trigger
  • Still a menu — actions are one click away, not zero
Option 4

Segmented action toolbar

Pill-shaped grouped buttons with both icon and label. Overflow ("⋯ More") catches the rest.

Ad Group 1 Total Variations: 9
  • Most discoverable — actions are spelled out
  • Heaviest visually; eats horizontal space
  • "More" handles long tails cleanly
  • Doesn't scale well to dense lists
Option 5

Hover-reveal toolbar

Card header stays clean — actions fade in only when the row is hovered (or focused via keyboard).

Ad Group 1 Total Variations: 9

↑ hover the card above to reveal actions

  • Cleanest at rest — no chrome until needed
  • Touch devices have no hover state
  • Scales beautifully to long lists
  • Less discoverable for first-time users
Option 6

Status toggle + slim "More"

The most-used action (pause/resume) lives as a labeled toggle. Everything else goes under a small "More" link.

Ad Group 1 Total Variations: 9
  • Status is visible and adjustable inline
  • Two controls instead of one
  • "More" keeps secondary noise out of the way
  • Only fits if status really is the top action

Quick recommendation

For a campaign builder with 5–7 actions and dense vertical layouts, Option 3 ("Actions" labeled dropdown) is the safest upgrade — it keeps the menu pattern (which scales), removes the ambiguity of the dots, and reads cleanly next to the collapse caret. If you want a more product-y feel and have the horizontal room, Option 5 (hover-reveal toolbar) is the most modern — see Linear, Notion, Vercel dashboard. Avoid Option 1 if the list grows long; avoid Option 4 if cards get narrow.
